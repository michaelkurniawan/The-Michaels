    • ISLE OF BERK,

    HOME OF THE VIKINGS

    At Berk, dragons went from challenge to cherished companions, making our island a place of endless wonder and friendship.

    894 AD, WHEN THE STORY BEGINS

    On the Isle of Berk, the Viking tribe lived in deep fear of dragons. They not only avoided the creatures but also waged war against them regularly. Sven, a young adventurous boy, wasn't entirely convinced that dragons were the true enemy.

    THE WAR BETWEEN TWO WORLDS

    Every day, Sven witnessed the battles between the Viking tribe and the dragons from a distance. Though forbidden to join the fight by his renowned warrior father, Sven remained curious about the dragons. One day, while hiding behind a large rock during a skirmish, he spotted a small wounded dragon abandoned by its pack.

    Without hesitation, Sven approached the injured dragon, whom he named "Blossom" because of the bright pink hue around its wings. Despite being afraid, Blossom felt a connection with Sven as he tended to its wounds. This unexpected friendship marked the beginning of a great change on the Isle of Berk.

    THE BOND GROWS DEEPER

    Sven and Blossom became inseparable. They roamed the island together, proving that not all dragons were enemies. However, Sven realized that to change the mindset of the people of Berk, they had to do more than just befriend dragons.

    THE ESTABLISHMENT PROCESS

    With the help of his friends, Sven attempted to persuade the tribe leaders to open their minds to the possibility of peace with dragons. Yet, their efforts were often thwarted by the deep-seated fear and hatred ingrained in the society.

    Undeterred, Sven and his friends devised a plan to establish the Zen Dragon Academy, a place where Vikings could learn to coexist with dragons. Despite facing opposition from some, the academy was eventually opened in the hope of changing the community's perception of dragons.

    ZEN DRAGON ACADEMY, THE SYMBOLS OF UNITY

    With unwavering determination and the spirit of friendship between Sven and Blossom, they worked hard to prove that peace between humans and dragons was possible. And eventually, the Isle of Berk became a place where both species lived together peacefully and harmoniously. Sven and Blossom, along with their friends, became symbols of unity and goodness among the Viking tribe and the dragons.
